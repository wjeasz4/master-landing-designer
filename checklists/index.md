# Checklists

## Purpose

`checklists/` holds concise verification instruments used after reasoning. A checklist protects a known quality gate; it does not choose a design direction.

Checklists support consistency where omissions are costly, especially in constitutional compliance, accessibility, performance, Decision Records, and design review.

## What Belongs Here

- constitutional compliance checks;
- Decision Record completeness checks;
- the twelve-dimension Design Review Framework;
- accessibility verification tied to defined strategies;
- performance budget verification;
- knowledge contribution checks; and
- release or repository integrity checks.

## What Does Not Belong Here

- ideation recipes;
- aesthetic scoring systems;
- universal layout rules;
- checks that cannot change acceptance or revision;
- duplicated standards without source links;
- long explanations that belong in `knowledge/`; or
- boxes that reward completion without evidence.

## Checklist Standard

Every checklist must state:

1. the gate it protects;
2. when it runs;
3. required evidence;
4. pass, refine, reconsider, and blocked conditions where applicable;
5. severity rules;
6. the decision or artifact that a failure returns to;
7. applicable standards or references; and
8. ownership and revision triggers.

Write checks as observable questions. “Looks premium” is not verifiable. “Does the primary action remain distinguishable without relying on color alone?” is reviewable.

## How Codex Should Use This Collection

Use a checklist only after the relevant reasoning and strategy exist. Record evidence for each material finding. Trace failure back to the earliest decision capable of resolving it.

Do not satisfy a checklist through wording changes when the underlying design remains weak. A constitutional failure blocks progress.

## Contribution Standard

A contribution must:

- comply with the [Repository Constitution](../CONSTITUTION.md);
- protect a recurring, consequential gate;
- avoid duplicating an authoritative standard;
- define evidence and outcomes clearly;
- remain short enough for reliable use; and
- explain how review findings improve future decisions.

## Relationship to the System

[Knowledge](../knowledge/index.md) explains why, [patterns](../patterns/index.md) support choice, and [templates](../templates/index.md) structure artifacts. Checklists verify that their outputs meet agreed requirements.
