# Knowledge

## Purpose

`knowledge/` holds durable design principles and conceptual models that help Codex explain why landing-page decisions work. Knowledge should remain useful after a visual trend, framework, or tool has changed.

This collection is the repository's explanatory layer. It describes forces such as attention, comprehension, trust, motivation, perceived effort, visual organization, reading behavior, accessibility, performance, and brand meaning.

## What Belongs Here

- evidence-backed design principles;
- perceptual, cognitive, behavioral, and communication models;
- accessibility and performance principles that shape design decisions;
- conceptual relationships between design dimensions;
- bounded expert knowledge with provenance and stated uncertainty; and
- durable corrections derived from repeated review findings.

## What Does Not Belong Here

- implementation instructions or framework APIs;
- trend reports without a durable principle;
- universal prescriptions detached from context;
- copied product analysis presented as a rule;
- prompt collections, templates, or checklists;
- isolated opinions without evidence; or
- examples whose main purpose is demonstration rather than explanation.

## Knowledge Document Contract

Every document must answer all eight questions:

1. **Why this principle exists:** What human, perceptual, cognitive, brand, technical, or business problem does it address?
2. **When to use it:** Which evidence and conditions make it relevant?
3. **When not to use it:** Which contexts, constraints, or competing priorities make it harmful or irrelevant?
4. **Trade-offs:** What improves, what weakens, and what does the choice cost?
5. **Psychological effect:** How might it affect attention, comprehension, trust, emotion, motivation, or perceived effort, and how strong is the evidence?
6. **Common beginner mistakes:** Which superficial applications miss the principle?
7. **Expert observations:** Which interactions, edge cases, or subtle signals matter in experienced practice?
8. **Decision heuristics:** Which conditional rules help Codex choose without becoming recipes?

A document that cannot answer all eight questions is not ready for this collection.

## Evidence Requirements

Distinguish:

- established evidence;
- observed evidence;
- expert practice;
- inference; and
- hypothesis.

Link claims to [references](../references/index.md) when external evidence or standards support them. State limitations and plausible counterexamples. Avoid certainty that exceeds the source.

## How Codex Should Use This Collection

Load a knowledge document only when a material decision needs its principle. Translate the principle into the current product and audience context. Do not copy its prior expression or treat its heuristic as an automatic answer.

Use relevant knowledge to strengthen the Rationale, Trade-offs, Risks, and Review Checklist of a [Decision Record](../templates/index.md).

## Contribution Standard

A contribution must:

- comply with the [Repository Constitution](../CONSTITUTION.md);
- satisfy the complete Knowledge Document Contract;
- state evidence strength and uncertainty;
- distinguish the principle from its possible visual expressions;
- identify interactions with other design dimensions;
- avoid duplicating an existing document; and
- define observable review signals.

## Relationship to the System

Knowledge explains why. [Patterns](../patterns/index.md) organize recurring contextual choices. [Examples](../examples/index.md) demonstrate reasoning. [Checklists](../checklists/index.md) verify completed reasoning. None of them may replace this collection's explanatory role.
