# References

## Purpose

`references/` holds external standards, research notes, citations, source summaries, and provenance records that support or challenge repository knowledge.

References make evidence inspectable. They do not become recommendations until their relevance, limitations, and context are reasoned through.

## What Belongs Here

- authoritative accessibility and web standards;
- primary research relevant to perception, cognition, behavior, or performance;
- official platform and browser documentation;
- carefully bounded secondary syntheses when primary sources are unavailable;
- source notes linking claims to specific evidence;
- provenance and licensing records; and
- records of conflicting evidence or unresolved questions.

## What Does Not Belong Here

- link dumps without claim mapping;
- trend roundups or inspiration galleries;
- marketing claims treated as research;
- copied articles or material that violates copyright;
- sources selected only because they support a preferred conclusion;
- expired links without an archived or replacement source; or
- implementation documentation unrelated to an active reasoning need.

## Source Quality

Evaluate each source for:

1. authority and relevant expertise;
2. publication date and temporal stability;
3. methodology or basis of the claim;
4. sample, population, and context limits;
5. conflicts of interest;
6. whether the source is primary, secondary, or interpretive;
7. agreement or conflict with other evidence; and
8. the exact repository claims it supports or challenges.

Separate quotation from paraphrase and inference. Respect source licenses and quotation limits.

## Claim Traceability

Reference notes should identify:

- the claim or decision they inform;
- the source title, author or organization, date, and stable location;
- the relevant finding in concise paraphrase;
- evidence strength and limitations;
- competing evidence; and
- a review date when the information can change.

Avoid citing a source for a broader claim than it supports.

## How Codex Should Use This Collection

Load references when a material claim needs support, a standard defines a requirement, or uncertainty could change a decision. Translate evidence into the current context and label inference explicitly.

Do not use citation count as a substitute for source quality. When evidence conflicts, preserve the disagreement and record how the decision handles it.

## Contribution Standard

A contribution must:

- comply with the [Repository Constitution](../CONSTITUTION.md);
- support or challenge a specific repository claim;
- prefer primary and authoritative sources;
- document limitations and temporal risk;
- preserve copyright and licensing boundaries;
- avoid duplication; and
- define when the reference should be reviewed or retired.

## Relationship to the System

References support [knowledge](../knowledge/index.md), contextualize [patterns](../patterns/index.md), validate [checklists](../checklists/index.md), and document [asset](../assets/index.md) provenance. They inform decisions but do not make them.
