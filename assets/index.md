# Assets

## Purpose

`assets/` will hold governed source materials used in future outputs. The foundation contains no production assets; this index defines the conditions any future asset must meet before inclusion.

An asset is not neutral. It affects originality, meaning, accessibility, performance, licensing, and brand consistency.

## What Belongs Here

- original or properly licensed images, illustrations, icons, type resources, and media;
- source files intended to be transformed into approved outputs;
- brand-owned materials with documented usage constraints;
- optimized variants whose relationship to the source is clear; and
- machine-readable provenance or attribution records when required.

## What Does Not Belong Here

- copied product assets or recreated brand signatures;
- files with unknown origin or incompatible licenses;
- scraped images without explicit rights;
- decorative assets without a decision rationale;
- unoptimized media that violates performance strategy;
- assets that communicate meaning without accessible alternatives; or
- framework boilerplate, finished pages, caches, and generated build output.

## Required Asset Record

Every asset contribution must document:

1. filename and purpose;
2. creator or source;
3. license and permitted uses;
4. required attribution;
5. modifications made;
6. brand or legal restrictions;
7. accessibility treatment, including alternatives when needed;
8. intrinsic dimensions, format, and expected delivery size;
9. optimization decisions; and
10. the Decision Record or design rationale authorizing its use.

## How Codex Should Use This Collection

Select assets only after visual language, composition, accessibility, and performance decisions exist. Verify the asset record before use. Prefer product-specific meaning over generic decoration.

Do not let the availability of an attractive asset determine the design direction. If rights, provenance, or accessible use are unclear, treat the asset as blocked.

## Contribution Standard

A contribution must:

- comply with the [Repository Constitution](../CONSTITUTION.md);
- have verifiable provenance and compatible rights;
- support a documented design decision;
- include accessibility and performance treatment;
- avoid redundant variants; and
- be removable without breaking the repository's knowledge architecture.

## Relationship to the System

Assets express approved decisions. [References](../references/index.md) preserve external provenance, [Decision Record templates](../templates/index.md) authorize use, and [checklists](../checklists/index.md) verify accessibility and performance.
