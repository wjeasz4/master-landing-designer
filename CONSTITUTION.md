# Repository Constitution

## Purpose

This Constitution is the highest authority in `master-landing-designer`. It governs every skill instruction, agent behavior, knowledge document, pattern, example, diagnostic prompt, template, checklist, asset policy, reference, contribution, and review.

The repository exists to develop Design Intelligence: the ability to understand a product and its audience, translate evidence into coherent design decisions, anticipate consequences, and review whether the resulting landing page serves people, brand, and business without sacrificing accessibility or performance.

## Authority

Repository guidance follows this order:

1. `CONSTITUTION.md`
2. `SKILL.md` and `AGENTS.md`
3. repository governance documents
4. collection indexes
5. individual knowledge artifacts

When guidance conflicts, the higher authority prevails. Correct the subordinate artifact. Never weaken, reinterpret, or bypass this Constitution through lower-level wording.

## Immutable Principles

### 1. Reason before implementation

Understand the product, audience, evidence, constraints, and intended effect before generating code or prescribing form. Code is the expression of a design decision, not a substitute for one.

### 2. Principles over trends

Evaluate trends through durable truths about people, perception, communication, systems, and technology. Popularity can reveal context, but it cannot prove fitness.

### 3. Evidence over opinion

Separate research, standards, observed behavior, expert practice, inference, and hypothesis. State the strength and limits of evidence. Do not present preference as fact.

### 4. Originality over imitation

Derive the design from product truth and audience needs. Learn from references by extracting principles; never reproduce another product's layout, typography, components, copy, motion, or signature treatment.

### 5. Systems over isolated components

Judge each choice by its effect on hierarchy, rhythm, meaning, behavior, accessibility, performance, brand, and conversion as a whole. Local polish must not damage system coherence.

### 6. Accessibility is mandatory

Treat inclusive perception, understanding, navigation, and interaction as design requirements from the first decision. Accessibility is neither optional nor deferred polish.

### 7. Performance is a feature

Treat speed, responsiveness, stability, and resource discipline as part of the experience and the brand promise. Visual ambition must operate within explicit performance constraints.

### 8. Every recommendation must explain why

Connect every recommendation to context, evidence, intended effect, trade-offs, and observable success criteria. Unexplained advice is not Design Intelligence.

### 9. Every pattern must document trade-offs

State what a pattern improves, what it may weaken, what it costs, when it fails, and which conditions change the recommendation. No pattern is universally correct.

### 10. Every review finding must improve future decisions

Trace findings to the decisions that produced them. When evidence is reusable, feed it back into knowledge, heuristics, checks, or skill behavior instead of treating review as one-time correction.

### 11. The repository is a living knowledge system

Revise subordinate knowledge when evidence changes. Preserve provenance, expose uncertainty, resolve contradictions, and prevent stale guidance from becoming doctrine.

## Constitutional Violations

A contribution or behavior violates this Constitution when it:

- copies or closely imitates an existing product's distinctive expression;
- provides generic AI design advice detached from product, audience, context, or evidence;
- presents unsupported claims, personal taste, or trend popularity as universal truth;
- recommends a decision without rationale, intended effect, trade-offs, or review criteria;
- introduces a pattern without contraindications, risks, or failure modes;
- contradicts higher-authority guidance or leaves conflicting guidance unresolved;
- generates code before completing product understanding, material decisions, accessibility, performance, synthesis, and review gates;
- treats accessibility or performance as optional, deferrable, or expendable;
- optimizes an isolated element while damaging the wider design system;
- adds prompts, templates, checklists, examples, or assets without increasing reusable Design Intelligence; or
- suppresses uncertainty, provenance, review findings, or evidence that should change future guidance.

Constitutional violations cannot be accepted as residual risk.

## Enforcement

Constitutional compliance is a blocking gate.

1. Identify the violated principle and the conflicting artifact or behavior.
2. Stop publication, merge, recommendation, or code generation while the violation is material.
3. Correct the subordinate guidance or decision; wording alone does not create an exception.
4. Record recurring violations as evidence for improving indexes, heuristics, checks, or skill behavior.
5. Use the amendment process when a proposal genuinely challenges an immutable principle.

Report constitutional violations separately from ordinary refinements so their authority and severity remain clear.

## Amendment Process

The principles are immutable under ordinary contribution. The repository may evolve without compromising them.

A constitutional amendment requires all of the following:

1. A dedicated Decision Record describing the context, problem, options, decision, rationale, trade-offs, risks, and review checklist.
2. Evidence that the existing constitutional language harms the repository's mission or prevents responsible adaptation.
3. An impact analysis covering `SKILL.md`, `AGENTS.md`, governance documents, collection indexes, and affected knowledge.
4. Explicit maintainer approval.
5. A changelog entry and versioned migration plan.
6. A focused change set that does not hide the amendment inside unrelated work.

If these conditions are not met, the Constitution remains unchanged.
